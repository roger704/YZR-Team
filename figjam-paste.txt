Tap to Trust

Designing the Approval Moment for Agentic AI

Superchat Design Challenge · Mobile-first · 10 seconds of trust

01 PROBLEM STATEMENT

AI agents are starting to take real actions on behalf of businesses — upgrading plans, processing refunds, booking appointments, updating customer records.

For high-stakes actions, the owner needs to stay in the loop. But "Approve / Deny" is nowhere near enough.

Design the mobile-first approval moment: the 10 seconds between the agent asking for the go-ahead and the owner tapping back.

Sometimes it's a quick yes.

Sometimes it's nuance — a cap, a condition, a whisper in the agent's ear.

Sometimes it's "let me handle this myself."

02 CONTEXT & BACKGROUND

Superchat is the communication OS for 9,000+ businesses globally.

WhatsApp, Email, Voice, and every other channel a customer reaches out on — unified in one place.

We're now building the next layer: AI agents that don't just chat, but act.

Triggering payments. Changing subscriptions. Booking slots. Updating records across connected tools.

From agents that talk → to agents that do. One of the biggest design frontiers in SaaS right now.

Nobody has figured out what it should feel like. This is your shot at defining it.

03 TARGET USER

The always-on, never-at-the-desk SMB owner.

Trusts their AI agent for most things — but for moments that matter, wants control without opening a laptop.

DENTAL PRACTICE OWNER — Between patients. Phone in pocket. 10 seconds between chair turnovers.

RESTAURANT MANAGER — Mid-shift. Hands full. Loud room. Maybe can't type at all.

PERSONAL TRAINER — Between sessions. Gym floor. One thumb. Sweat on screen.

05 THE APPROVAL FLOW

CUSTOMER — Messages on WhatsApp: "Can I get a refund?"

AI AGENT — Detects high-stakes action, requests approval with context

OWNER — Receives push notification, 10-second window to decide

ACTION — Executed in the connected tool

MEANWHILE, CUSTOMER SIDE — "Let me double-check that with Dr. Marquez — one sec."

⏱ 10-second window

06 THE RESPONSE SPECTRUM

APPROVE — Quick yes. Agent proceeds.

CONSTRAIN — "Go, but cap at €50"

MODIFY — Edit the action itself, then approve.

WHISPER — "Do it, but ask them about X first"

TAKEOVER — "I'll handle this myself"

DENY — No, with a reason the agent can use gracefully.

DEFER — "Ask me again in 10 min"

07 WHAT GREAT LOOKS LIKE

CONTEXT, NOT CLUTTER — The owner sees exactly what they need: action, customer, history, risk. Nothing more. Sometimes a lot. Sometimes almost nothing. Knowing the difference is the craft.

THE CUSTOMER SIDE — What does the customer see while the owner decides? Silence is a design choice. So is "let me double-check for you."

THE HANDOFF BACK — Once the owner decides, the agent continues confidently, in-character, without breaking the conversation. The seam should be invisible.

EMOTIONAL TONE — Light. Fast. Trusted. The owner keeps sipping their coffee.

08 BONUS POINTS

FAILURE MODES — What if the owner doesn't respond?

LEARNING LOOPS — Does the agent get smarter from each decision?

VOICE-FIRST — True hands-free interaction.

SCOPE TIP

Focus deeply on ONE realistic scenario (plan upgrade, refund, booking change) and design it end-to-end.

Depth beats breadth.
